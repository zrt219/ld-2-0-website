# Lornette Daye Homepage Market Research Notes

Last updated: 2026-05-18

## Homepage Positioning Goal
The homepage should sell Lornette Daye as a premium transformational speaker whose core message is:

> Transforming the lives of others that they can overcome anything because they matter.

The page should help an event planner quickly answer:
- What is Lornette's message?
- Who is she for?
- What outcomes does she create?
- Can I see her presence on video?
- What credible proof is available without overclaiming?
- How do I book or review speaker materials?

## Reference URLs

1. Mic Drop Workshop: `https://micdropworkshop.com/amplify/5-things-your-speaker-website-needs`
   - Notes: Speaker websites need strong speaker imagery, a keynote abstract, client/proof assets when legitimately available, testimonials, and one-liner quotes from the speaker's own message.
   - Lornette application: Put her main message and speaker reel near the top. Keep testimonials clearly labeled as supplied brand materials. Do not invent proof logos.

2. NanoGlobals public speaker website examples: `https://nanoglobals.com/public-speaking-websites/`
   - Notes: High-converting speaker sites use clear keynote pages, visible booking paths, named credible proof, and downloadable one-sheets/speaker kits.
   - Lornette application: Homepage should expose "Speaker", "Books", "Media", and "Book Lornette" paths without making planners hunt.

3. Speakers Reels: `https://www.speakersreels.ca/`
   - Notes: Selection committees make fast decisions from reel content. A reel should show stage presence, story, and human connection, and it should be concise.
   - Lornette application: Treat the reel as a primary homepage proof asset. Include short clip slots for Road to the Olympics, navigating change and disappointment, and staying grounded after adversity as the media library grows.

4. Solhaus Media speaker reel guidance: `https://www.solhausmedia.com/speaker-reel-production`
   - Notes: A reel should answer what the speaker will deliver for the audience. Event organizers book based on impact, clarity, and trust, not only production value.
   - Lornette application: Pair video areas with audience outcomes, not only titles. The homepage should make the transformation promise explicit.

5. Steve Lowisz speaker homepage: `https://stevelowisz.com/`
   - Notes: Strong speaker homepage pattern: direct headline, booking CTA, watch-reel CTA, reel stats/proof, signature keynotes, testimonials, and final availability CTA.
   - Lornette application: Use a video-forward hero, a planner-ready proof strip, signature audience pathways, and repeat "Book Lornette" at decision points.

6. Crash Design Co speaker website checklist: `https://crashdesign.co/what-should-be-on-a-speaker-website-and-what-you-might-be-missing/`
   - Notes: Common weak spots include no clear keynote titles, no audience outcomes, outdated/missing video, hidden testimonials, weak brand design, and no obvious booking step.
   - Lornette application: Put outcome language near every speaking pathway and keep the booking route one click away.

7. BigSpeak Speakers Bureau: `https://www.bigspeak.com/`
   - Notes: Speaker bureau environments make categories, topics, videos, and booking/contact actions easy to scan for professional buyers.
   - Lornette application: Keep speaker, media, books, recognition, and booking pathways visible without making the homepage feel like a proof dump.

8. Speaker homepage reference, Steve Lowisz: `https://stevelowisz.com/`
   - Notes: Strong speaker homepages make the offer, watch-reel action, topic fit, and booking path clear before deeper scrolling.
   - Lornette application: Use the homepage as the gateway into the Speaker, Media, Books, and Book Lornette sections.

## Homepage Strategy For Lornette

### Above The Fold
- Lead with the main message, not a long credential list.
- Keep one credential cluster visible: national sprint champion, national coach, author, diversity award winner.
- Show the speaker reel or a premium reel placeholder alongside the hero.
- Use primary CTA: `Book Lornette`.
- Use secondary CTA: `Watch Speaker Reel`.

### Interactive Selling Section
Use an audience-pathway interface so planners can self-identify:
- Event Planners
- Schools & Youth
- Faith & Community
- Corporate & Leadership
- Athletes & Coaches

Each pathway should show:
- recommended message/topic
- transformation promise
- audience outcomes
- CTA to the most relevant page

### Proof Without Fabrication
Safe proof:
- User-provided testimonials
- Supplied recognition artwork
- Authored books
- YouTube links supplied by user
- Past engagement names supplied by user
- Toastmasters/Rotary assets supplied in project files

Avoid:
- Fake press logos
- Fake client logos
- Fake media features
- "Olympian" claims
- Exact audited numbers unless clearly labeled as brand-provided

### Booking Path
Repeat booking CTAs at natural decision points:
- hero
- video/reel section
- audience pathways
- final CTA

Keep planner assets visible:
- Media
- Speaker Kit
- Books
- Recognition
- Contact/booking form

## Reusable Improvement Checklist
When asked to "improve the homepage", review these items first:
- Is the main message visible without scrolling?
- Does the page show Lornette's presence on video above or near the fold?
- Can an event planner find topics, outcomes, proof, and booking in under 30 seconds?
- Are all proof claims grounded in supplied assets?
- Is the mobile page free of cramped cards, tiny text, and horizontal overflow?
- Are CTAs repeated but not noisy?
- Does each section answer a buyer question instead of only decorating the page?
